How Staking Interest Works: 4 Stages

1

Protocol issues block rewards

The network creates new tokens and/or distributes transaction fees to validators. This is the gross source of staking interest.

2

Validator deducts commission

Before sharing rewards, validators take a cut — typically 3–15%. Commission is the most directly controllable drag on your rate.

3

Rewards reach your stake

Your share of the reward pool is proportional to your stake. Rewards may accumulate on-chain until you claim them.

4

Claim, compound, or exit

Claiming costs gas. Compounding grows future interest but adds more gas. Exiting triggers an unbonding period that pauses accrual.

APY vs APR: Which Number to Trust

The difference between APY and APR is compounding — and in staking, compounding is never free.

APY vs APR Net Yield Compounding Cost
MetricDefinitionWhen it overstates real returns
APRSimple annualised rate, no compoundingWhen you assume compounding happens automatically
APYAnnualised rate with compounding baked inWhen compounding frequency is low or fees eat the gain
Net APRAPR minus commission and tx costsRarely — this is the number to target

Rule: Always ask for net APR after commission and fees. APY is a marketing figure until you verify the compounding frequency and transaction cost assumptions.

What drives your rate?

  • Network inflation schedule — protocol issuance rules set the ceiling.
  • Total staked ratio — more tokens staked = lower per-token rate.
  • Validator commission — directly subtracted from your share.
  • Transaction fee revenue — some networks add fee income on top of issuance.
  • Slashing events — rare but can reduce principal for affected delegators.

Tax Treatment of Staking Interest (2025–2026 Overview)

Tax treatment varies by jurisdiction and is actively evolving. This is not tax advice. For official guidance see IRS (US) and HMRC (UK).

Tax Basics Record-Keeping Jurisdictions
  • Income at receipt: Most jurisdictions treat staking rewards as ordinary income at fair market value when received.
  • Capital gains on sale: Subsequent appreciation is typically a capital gains event.
  • Compounding complexity: Each claim event may be a separate taxable income event.
  • Liquid staking tokens: Wrapping/unwrapping (stETH → ETH) may be a taxable disposal in some jurisdictions.
  • Loss events: Slashing losses may be deductible — verify locally.
Minimum record-keeping: Date of each reward claim, amount in tokens, USD/EUR value at receipt, gas fees paid. Tools: Koinly, CoinTracker, TokenTax.

Best Practices: Maximising Staking Interest Safely

  • Use liquid staking for small balances — auto-compounding eliminates per-claim gas drag.
  • Pick validators by reliability, not headline rate — commission stability and uptime matter more than +0.5% APR.
  • Keep a gas buffer — always hold enough native token to claim, unstake, or exit.
  • Know your unbonding period before you stake — illiquidity at the wrong time is a hidden cost.
  • Review allowances periodically — revoke stale permissions at revoke.cash.
  • Track realised outcomes quarterly — adjust if net yield diverges from expectations.
  • Use a hardware wallet for meaningful positions.
Most common mistake: Optimising for +1% higher APY while ignoring 10× larger risks — smart contract exposure, validator opacity, or poor wallet hygiene.

Troubleshooting: Common Issues & Fixes

"My staking interest is lower than advertised"

  • The advertised rate was APY; you're earning APR (fewer compounds than assumed).
  • Validator commission is higher than expected — recheck the current rate.
  • Network total staked increased, diluting per-token rewards.
  • Token price declined — USD return is lower even if token APR is unchanged.

"Rewards are not appearing in my wallet"

  • For rebasing tokens (stETH), check wallet settings — some wallets hide rebase changes.
  • You may need to claim rewards manually — check the protocol's UI.
  • Validator has temporary downtime — verify on the protocol's validator dashboard.

"I can't unstake / withdraw"

  • Unbonding period is still active — wait for completion.
  • A claim/finalise step is required before withdrawal.
  • Insufficient gas for the exit transaction — top up your native token balance.
Best debugging method: Always check on-chain data first — UI displays can lag. Use Beaconcha.in for Ethereum validator state.

Frequently Asked Questions

Staking interest is compensation earned for locking tokens to support a proof-of-stake network. Unlike bank interest (contractual, insured, regulated), it is protocol-issued, variable, token-denominated, and carries smart contract, validator, and market risks with no deposit insurance.

Rates vary widely: Ethereum via Lido ~3–4% APR; Solana ~6–7%; Cosmos (ATOM) ~10–14%; Polkadot ~12–15%. All rates are nominal in token terms — your USD return depends on token price performance.

In most major jurisdictions (US, UK, Australia, Germany) staking rewards are treated as ordinary income at fair market value when received. Subsequent appreciation is a capital gain. Always consult a local tax professional.

Lido pools ETH across a diversified set of professional validators. Rewards accrue daily and are reflected in your stETH balance via rebasing — your balance increases daily proportionally to the network staking rate minus Lido's 10% fee. No manual claiming required.

APY assumes compounding and ignores costs. Real yield is what you actually receive after commission, fees, and inflation adjustment. A 12% APY can be 6% net APR in practice — and if the token depreciates 20%, your real return is negative.

It depends on the method. Native staking requires manual claims and restaking, each costing gas. Liquid staking tokens like stETH compound automatically via rebasing — balance grows daily with no action required.

Main risks: smart contract vulnerabilities, validator slashing, token price depreciation, peg/liquidity risk for derivative tokens, and regulatory uncertainty. Using well-audited, established protocols significantly reduces but does not eliminate risk.

Lido accepts any ETH amount (no 32 ETH requirement). Your practical minimum is the balance where transaction fees don't consume your interest income — roughly $500–$1,000 for native staking to be fee-efficient.

Your optimal frequency is where the APY gain exceeds the gas cost. For balances under ~$5,000, monthly or never (using liquid staking) is usually the best net outcome. For larger positions on low-fee networks, weekly may be worth it.
